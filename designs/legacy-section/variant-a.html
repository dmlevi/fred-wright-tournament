LII Annual · Anno Domini MMXXVI
In Honor Of
“A REMEMBRANCE OF FRED” Frederick J. Wright Jr. (1898–1958) was an amateur of the first rank — a member of the winning 1923 United States Walker Cup team, seven-time Massachusetts state champion, and the 1956 USGA Senior Amateur champion. He was still competing for the National Amateur on August 26, 1958, when he collapsed at the thirteenth tee of The Country Club at Brookline. This tournament is played each spring in his memory.
— The Board of Governors
A Life In Golf

Hall of Memory

Five moments from the archive — from a young Wright on the United States team at Hoylake through his prime at Oakley and into living memory at the Club.

1921
MCMXXI

America v. Great Britain

At Royal Liverpool’s Hoylake links, the informal match between America and Great Britain — the contest that would formalize, the next year, as the Walker Cup. Wright (fourth from left) played for the United States alongside Bobby Jones, Francis Ouimet, and Jesse Guilford under captain William C. Fownes Jr., paired in the singles against Britain’s E. W. Holderness.

Oakley Archives, MCMXXI
1923
MCMXXIII

A Walker Cup Won

Two years on, the United States team aboard the liner for St. Andrews and the second Walker Cup: Ouimet, Gardner, Rotan, Willing, Johnson, Herron. Wright was a member of the winning American side; the United States took the cup 6½–5½. The photograph carries Ouimet’s hand.

Signed by Ouimet · Oakley Archives, MCMXXIII
c.1920s
MCMXX–XXIX

Among Champions

Wright (right) with Jess Guilford, Francis Ouimet, and Roland Hancock — four amateurs of national rank. Guilford was the 1921 U.S. Amateur champion; Ouimet, the 1913 U.S. Open champion at Brookline.

Oakley Archives, c. MCMXX–XXIX
1948
MCMXLVIII

An Ace on the Fourteenth

An afternoon at Oakley, the ball in the cup at the fourteenth. Wright posed with the flagstick the way a man poses for a photograph he expects his grandchildren to see.

Oakley Archives, c. MCMXLVIII
2019
MMXIX

The Portrait Returns

Wright’s granddaughters with the oil portrait donated by the Wright family to the Club. The painting hangs at Oakley.

Oakley Archives, MMXIX
